# Reporting, Analytics, and Data Quality

## Why This Module Matters to You

This module helps you perform reporting, analytics, and data quality tasks in a way that is consistent, evidence-based, and safe for live Moodle users. The focus is on what you need to do, what to check, and how to explain your decisions.

## What You Will Be Able to Do

- Use Moodle reports and logs to answer common support questions.
- Recognize report filter, date, timezone, and completion criteria issues.
- Identify data quality problems that affect administration decisions.
- Prepare concise evidence for academic and support stakeholders.

## Topics You Will Practice

- Logs and activity reports.
- Completion and participation evidence.
- Inactive courses and users.
- Data quality checks.
- Report interpretation notes.

## Hands-On Lab

Interpret sample report outputs and write an evidence-based response to a learner or instructor support request.

## Your Evidence of Completion

By the end of this module, you should have:

- A completed checklist or decision note.
- Screenshots or written observations from a non-production Moodle site where available.
- A short explanation of the risk, validation method, and next action.

## Key Takeaways

- Intermediate administration is about repeatable workflows, not just knowing where menus are.
- Every meaningful change should have an owner, rationale, validation method, and rollback path.
- Logs, reports, and stakeholder confirmation should be used together before declaring an issue resolved.
